The Shark Fin Trade

Yokohama, Japan

The following information can be found at this website. Every day at the Japanese fishing port, Kesennuma City Port, about 40-70 tons of blue shark is laid out on the dock. The fisherman cut off the fins from the sharks and throw the rest of the shark back into the ocean. Why? There is a popular demand for shark fin soup because it is considered a noble and expensive meal. This is not a sustainable way to quench the population's need for shark fin soup. Given that it takes blue sharks several years to reach full maturity, they do not reproduce often. Therefore, killing them at a rate this fast will promise the extinction of blue sharks in the near future. In 2019, the United States passed a law that made it illegal to buy or sell shark fins. This is a great step in the right direction. Some things you can do to help are:

  • Do not order shark fin soup.
  • Boycott resturaunts or stores that sell shark fins.
  • Help spread the word. Not many people know about this problem.
